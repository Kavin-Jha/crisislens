The Problem

The feedback gap in crisis training

The 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline handled over 5 million contacts in 2023. The volunteers who answered those calls were, by most measures, undertrained and under-supported.

Supervisors stretched across dozens of trainees cannot listen to every practice call. Feedback is sparse. And the mistakes that miss a buried signal go uncaught — at the worst possible cost.

CrisisLens fills that gap. A trainee submits a simulated practice transcript and receives structured, clinically grounded coaching feedback in seconds.

How It Works
01
Risk Detection
Scans every exchange for language mapping to Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) criteria — including veiled, embedded, and farewell-pattern signals.
02
Confidence Scoring
Rates each flagged signal HIGH, MEDIUM, or LOW with clinical reasoning. A system that treats all flags identically is dangerous — this one doesn't.
03
MI Evaluation
Scores volunteer responses against SAMHSA Motivational Interviewing principles — reflective listening, open-ended questions, appropriate escalation.
04
Coaching Feedback
Generates exchange-level coaching notes ordered by clinical severity, with specific recommended responses and ranked alternatives for every critical miss.

Primary Evaluation Metric

Built to catch silent failures

A silent failure is when the system produces a clean, professional-sounding coaching report — "great active listening, minor areas for improvement" — while completely missing that the caller signaled suicidal ideation three separate times.

Red-Team Mode generates adversarial transcripts with deliberately buried risk signals — signals designed to hide inside casual, cheerful conversation. Then it measures recall rate: what percentage did the pipeline actually catch?

This is how you separate a system that works in demos from one that works in the real world.

Clinical Frameworks

Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale

cssrs.columbia.edu · Publicly available
Passive Ideation — wishes to be dead, no plan
Active Ideation — thinking about suicide, no method
Ideation + Plan — has a method in mind
Preparatory Behavior — taking steps toward attempt
Veiled Signal — indirect language suggesting ideation
Hopelessness Marker — clinically significant loss of meaning

Motivational Interviewing Principles

SAMHSA Guidelines · Public domain
Reflective Listening — accurately reflects caller's emotional state
Open-Ended Questions — invites elaboration, not yes/no
Affirmation — acknowledges caller's strengths
Avoid Leading — does not suggest answers or minimize
Appropriate Escalation — explores risk signals, does not pivot away
No Premature Solutions — listens fully before suggesting anything
Synthetic Data Only
No real crisis call transcripts are used at any point. All data is synthetic, modeled on publicly available SAMHSA training materials.
No PII Stored
Session data exists only in memory during a local run. Nothing is persisted to any external database. Privacy risk is low by design, not assumption.
Honest Uncertainty
The confidence scoring layer explicitly flags ambiguous signals. The system is engineered to be honest about what it knows and what it doesn't.
Training Tool Only
CrisisLens does not interact with real callers. It does not replace supervisors. It sharpens the people trying to help.
